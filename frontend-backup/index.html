About Detective Conan

Detective Conan (名探偵コナン, Meitantei Konan), also known as Case Closed in English-speaking countries, is a Japanese detective manga series written and illustrated by Gosho Aoyama. The story follows the adventures of a teenage detective who is transformed into a child while investigating a mysterious organization.

The Story Begins

Shinichi Kudo is a brilliant high school detective who frequently assists the police in solving complex cases. During an investigation, he is ambushed by members of a crime syndicate known as the Black Organization. They force-feed him an experimental poison intended to kill him, but instead of causing death, the drug mysteriously shrinks his body to that of a six-year-old child.

A New Identity

To protect his identity and continue his investigation into the Black Organization, Shinichi adopts the pseudonym Conan Edogawa. He moves in with his childhood friend Ran Mouri and her father, Kogoro Mouri, who runs a private detective agency. While maintaining his cover as an elementary school student, Conan secretly solves cases by using his brilliant deductive reasoning.

The Detective Method

Conan's unique approach involves using Dr. Agasa's special tranquilizer watch to sedate Kogoro Mouri, then employing a voice-changing bowtie to present his deductions through the sleeping detective. This clever ruse allows him to solve cases while keeping his true identity hidden. He also forms the Detective Boys group with his elementary school classmates, who often assist in his investigations.

Key Characters & Allies

Throughout his journey, Conan encounters numerous allies including Heiji Hattori, a fellow teenage detective from Osaka; Ai Haibara, a former Black Organization member who created the shrinking drug; and various police detectives from different regions. He also crosses paths with the mysterious phantom thief Kaito Kid, adding complexity to his dual life as both child and detective.

A Global Phenomenon

Since its debut in 1994, Detective Conan has become one of Japan's most beloved and long-running series, spanning over 1000 anime episodes, 27 feature films, numerous OVAs, and ongoing manga publications. The series has captivated audiences worldwide with its intricate mysteries, compelling character development, and the eternal theme that "the truth is always one."
